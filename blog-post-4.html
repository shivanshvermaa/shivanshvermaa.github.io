Blood Cell Detection using Deep Neural Networks

By Shivansh Verma

The determination of WBC type and blood cell count is crucial in clinical medical diagnostics. Therefore, it is highly desirable to automate the counting of blood cells and the classification of WBCs. The objective of this project is to develop a high-performance machine learning model that can recognize WBC types and detect blood cell components.

The project employs different Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) models such Inception model, ResNet50 and a custom implementation of CNN on BCCD (Blood Cell Count and Detection) Dataset, which is a small-scale dataset for blood cells detection licensed under MIT. The Inception model, with a 99.15% accuracy score, outperformed the other models in classification of the WBC type, according to the results. Even though the CNN has one-fourth fewer parameters than the Inception and ResNet50 model, it still managed to outperform ResNet50 with a relatively high accuracy of 98.34%. With a mAP (mean average precision) of 92.86%, YOLOv5's findings for identifying the blood cell components RBC, WBC, and Platelets were likewise excellent.

YOLOv5 model detecting the objects(WBCs,RBCs and Platelets) with their confidence levels.

TL;DR

• Designed a Custom CNN with just 142,980 parameters and a validation accuracy of 98.34%.
• Used YOLOv5 to detect WBCs, RBCs and Platelets with an mAP of 92.6%.
• Custom CNN outperformed ResNet50 and Inception models with 1/4th the number of paramertes from ResNet or Inception.
